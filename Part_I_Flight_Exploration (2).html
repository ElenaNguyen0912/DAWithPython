Part I - Flights¶

by Nguyen Thuy Thu Tam¶

Introduction¶

Flights dataset contains more than 1 million rowns of data relating to each flight in Jan and Feb of 2018. The dataset mainly focuses on describing the time compliance of the flight. Specifically whether flights depart or arrive as schedule time or not. Is there any reason for late departure or late arrival. The total time flights took on air and the actual distance of flights also included.

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

There are more than 1 million different flights in January and Feburary of 2008 with departured time, elapsed time, aired time, arrived time and the time these flights have been delayed for 5 main reasons: Carrier, NAS, Weather, Late Aircraft and Security. Besides, the original and destination timezone or the distance of the flights also included in the dataset.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

Actually this is my first time working with data relating to airlines. I found it intesting and eagered to explore what reason most impact the time compliance of the flights and what the maximum delayed time is within the time provided.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

I have to say that the data is quite clean and consolidated, almost columns in the dataset do play a specific role in my analysis and in my interest point as well. However, columns containing time information like departure_time, arrive_time, elapsed_time and 5 columns of delaying reasons are expected to help me investigate my features of interest.
